Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class

PROS

  1. Respectably Quick Car
      Although you have access to a more potent model, the base 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class has ample pep. Its boosted four-cylinder engine pushes out a tad more than 200 horsepower. A 0-60 time of 6.3 seconds makes the CLA-Class almost as quick as a V6-powered Lexus GS 350. You'll find the CLA 250 to be especially snappy around town. If you want to further sharpen up the sedan's performance, select the Sport mode.

  2. Handles More Like a Sports Sedan
      Despite being touted as an entry-level luxury car, the 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class drives more like a sports sedan. A poised suspension helps the CLA-Class to feel very athletic. You can really have fun on a bending road. Most cars in this price range don't handle as sharp. For folks who desire a softer ride, Mercedes-Benz offers an adjustable suspension. Drivers will also appreciate the sedan's tight turning radius.

  3. Good Fuel Economy
      All versions of the 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class have to be fed premium unleaded gas. Nevertheless, gas mileage is good for a vehicle of its performance caliber. According to the federal government's test data, the CLA 250 will return an inspiring 24 mpg in traffic and up to 37 mpg on the highway. Even the beastly AMG CLA 45 model can deliver 23 mpg in town and 30 mpg on the open road.

    CONS

  1. Short on Backseat Room
      In comparison to some of today's other luxury sedans, the CLA-Class feels a bit tight on the inside. While passengers sitting in the front are unlikely to have any issues, those riding in the back must sacrifice a great deal of headroom. A sloping room also makes accessing the rear seat a bit difficult. Expect the 2019 Audi A3 to be a bit roomier overall.

  2. Transmission Not Smooth At Low Speeds
      If you happen to be traveling on the highway, you'll have no issues with the sedan's dutch-clutch automatic transmission. However, a few issues may arise at low speeds. Some drivers have found that it's slow to shift at times. While the transmission may not be a deal breaker, it's something to watch out for during your test drive.
